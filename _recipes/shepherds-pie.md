---
title: Shepherd's Pie
author: Gaelan Lloyd
yield: Makes 4-5 long skinny Pyrex dishes.
ingredientsToBuy:
  - { qty: "5 lbs", item: "Ground beef" }

  - { qty: "2", item: "Onions (golden)" }
  - { qty: "3 lbs", item: "Frozen peas, carrots, corn (cubed)" }
  - { qty: "2 cans", item: "Campbell's condensed mushroom soup" }
  - { qty: "1.5 cups", item: "Chicken broth" }
  - { qty: "6 oz can", item: "Tomato paste" }
equipment:
  - { type: "5", item: "1.5 qt long skinny Pyrex" }
  - { type: "Large", item: "Grey Mixing bowl" }
  - { type: "2", item: "Large Staub pots" }
ingredientsYouHave:
  - Canola oil
  - Flour
  - Worcestershire sauce
  - Tomato paste
  - Garlic
  - Rosemary
  - Thyme
  - Bread crumbs
---
- Prep:
  - Make broth/chicken stock
  - Get out Rosemary/Thyme
  - Chop onions
- Heat the pots with oil or butter over medium-high heat.
- Add the onion. Saute with salt and pepper.
- Add the beef evenly between the pots and mix in a total of:
  - Garlic (1 tsp)
  - Salt (2 tsp)
  - Pepper (2 tsp)
- Cook until meat is evenly browned.
- Then, mix in to each pot:
  - Cooking wine
  - 4 tbsp flour
- Transfer the beef into the larger pot.
- Then, mix in:
  - Tomato paste
  - Mushroom soup
  - Chicken broth
  - Worcestershire sauce
  - Rosemary (4 tsp)
  - Thyme (4 tsp)
- Heat until bubbling, then reduce to simmer and cover for 15 min.
- Heat the oven on Convection at 375 degrees.
- Place baking sheets in oven to catch drips.
- Mix in the frozen corn and peas.
- Make the mashed potatoes.
- Spray the casserole dishes with Pam.
- Spread the mixture evenly into the casserole dishes and press it down.
- Top with mashed potatoes. Using a spatula, put large clumps down. Gently smooth over the potatoes, pulling towards the edges and pressing down. Create a seal at the edges to prevent the mixture from bubbling over.
- Cover with bread crumbs (mixed in butter, sprayed with Pam?)
- Spray the potato surface with Pam to help crisping.
- Bake for 25 min until the potatoes begin to brown.
- Allow to cool ~15 min before serving.
